The nanoscale refers to the size range between approximately 1 to 100 nanometers (nm), where materials and structures exhibit unique properties and behaviors due to their small size. At this scale, quantum effects become significant, and materials may have different optical, electronic, and mechanical properties compared to their bulk counterparts. Manipulating matter at the nanoscale enables the creation of novel materials, devices, and systems with enhanced functionalities, impacting fields such as medicine, electronics, and materials science.
